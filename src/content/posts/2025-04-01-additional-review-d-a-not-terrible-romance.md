## <u>The Hating Game by Sally Thorne</u>

![](/images/ghost/2025/04/download.png)

This was fairly readable and I actually liked both protagonists. They didn't just magically get together at the end and there was real chemistry. Oh my, the elevator scene, which I just so happened to get to while watching my daughter's soccer class! Do you ever close up the book as you're reading so no one can possibly see any words over your shoulder?

I loved that because they got the physical attraction out of the way towards the beginning we were able to see Lucy supporting Joshua at the wedding. I actually believe that these two are going to make it long-term as a couple. This is almost unheard of in romance books, the relationships often feel like they are build on sand.

The only reason it took me so long to finish was because it lived with my camping chair in the car and I only read it while waiting at soccer, swimming or school pick-up. It was actually pretty ok.

### Not bad. Hot in places 6/10
